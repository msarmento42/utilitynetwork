# Weekly Meal Planning Workflow

## What You'll Accomplish

Planning a week's worth of meals can feel overwhelming, but with a little AI help you'll create a **weekly dinner plan** that fits your schedule and taste. In this workflow, you'll harness an AI assistant (like ChatGPT) to quickly generate a balanced **7-day meal plan** and a matching grocery list. The goal is to save time on busy weeknights and ensure you have all ingredients on hand, *without* the nightly scramble of "what's for dinner?".

By the end of this workflow, you'll have:
- A clear **meal schedule** for the week (e.g., Monday through Sunday dinners).
- An **organized grocery list** covering everything needed.
- Meal ideas tailored to your dietary preferences and time constraints.

## Who This Is For

This workflow is perfect for **busy individuals and families** who want to streamline dinnertime. If you:
- Find yourself spending too much time each day deciding what to cook.
- Want to avoid takeout and cook at home more regularly.
- Have specific dietary preferences (vegetarian, gluten-free, etc.) or picky eaters to accommodate.
- Need to balance **budget and time** – for example, quick meals on work nights and maybe one special dinner on a weekend.

Use this at the **start of each week** (or over the weekend) to plan ahead. It's especially useful if you do a weekly grocery run and want to make sure nothing is forgotten.

## Inputs You Need

Before you start prompting your AI assistant, gather a few details:
- **Dietary preferences or requirements:** List any cuisines you love (Italian, Mexican) or foods to avoid (allergies, intolerances, dislikes).
- **Number of meals and people:** Usually 7 dinners, but maybe you only need 5 if you plan for leftovers or dining out. Note how many people each meal serves (for portion planning).
- **Time constraints:** Identify which days you'll have limited time. For example, maybe Mondays you need a 20-minute meal, whereas Wednesday you can slow cook chili.
- **Existing ingredients:** Optional but helpful – check your pantry and fridge for items you can incorporate (saving money and avoiding waste).
- **Budget (optional):** If you have a rough budget, you can mention it so the AI suggests cost-effective meals.

## Step-by-Step Workflow

### Step 1: Start the Conversation with Context
Begin by telling the AI your goal. *Example:* "I want to plan dinners for the next 7 days for a family of 4."

### Step 2: Provide Preferences and Constraints
In the same prompt (or a follow-up), give the details from your inputs. *Include:* dietary needs ("2 vegetarian meals, no shellfish"), time constraints ("Tues & Thu need quick prep"), and any ingredients to use ("have chicken, lots of rice").

### Step 3: Request a Meal Plan
Ask the AI clearly for a plan. E.g., "Please generate a day-by-day dinner plan for Mon–Sun that meets these requirements, with each meal idea and a brief description."

### Step 4: Ask for a Grocery List
Once the AI gives you meal suggestions, prompt it for an itemized grocery list. It helps to specify format: "List all ingredients I need to buy for these meals, organized by section (produce, dairy, etc.)."

### Step 5: Review and Refine
Read through the AI's meal plan. Does each meal look good? Are the recipes realistic for you? If something is off (too spicy for the kids, or too complex), tell the AI and ask for a tweak: "Replace Tuesday's meal with something kid-friendly, like tacos, using similar ingredients if possible."

### Step 6: Finalize the Plan
Confirm the final menu and grocery list. Save or print the list for your shopping trip, and maybe stick the meal plan on your fridge.

## Prompt Templates

- **Basic weekly plan:** "Generate a 7-day dinner plan for a family of 4. We prefer Italian, Mexican, and American cuisine, need two vegetarian meals, and nothing with peanuts. Provide meal ideas for each day and a grocery list of ingredients needed."
- **Include time info:** "Plan 5 weeknight dinners for two people. I only have about 30 minutes to cook on Mondays and Wednesdays; on other days I have more time. Include quick recipes on the busy days and more involved ones on free days."
- **Pantry integration:** "I want to use what I have. I have chicken thighs, a can of black beans, and a lot of rice. Create a meal plan for the next 4 dinners that uses these items so they don't go to waste, and list additional ingredients I'll need."
- **Budget-conscious:** "Make a weekly dinner plan that feeds 3 people on a $100 budget. Use affordable ingredients (like beans, ground turkey, seasonal veggies) and give an estimated cost for each meal's groceries."
- **Healthy variety:** "Create a week of dinners with a variety: e.g., one pasta night, one seafood, one vegetarian, etc. Keep it healthy but family-friendly. Provide the meal plan and shopping list."

## Common Mistakes

- **Too vague in prompts:** If you just say "plan my meals," the AI might give a generic or unsuitable plan. **Fix:** Include details like cuisine preferences, time limits, and ingredients you want to use or avoid.
- **Ignoring the plan's details:** Sometimes AI might suggest a meal that doesn't fit (like a long recipe on a busy night). **Fix:** Don't hesitate to tell the AI to swap it out or simplify it.
- **Not verifying the grocery list:** AI may list everything including items you already own, or miss items. **Fix:** Cross-check the list against your pantry.
- **Overlooking dietary needs:** If you don't mention an allergy or diet, the plan might include something you can't have. **Fix:** Always state critical restrictions in the prompt.

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] Variety & balance: Does the plan include different proteins or cuisines?
- [ ] Realistic for your schedule: Quick meals on busy days, more elaborate ones when you have time.
- [ ] Complete grocery list: Covers all recipes, grouped by section.
- [ ] Fits dietary needs: Every meal respects stated preferences.
- [ ] Allergy caution: Double-check no ingredient is something someone is allergic to.
