# Personal Knowledge Base Workflow (Notes → Summaries)

## What You'll Accomplish
By the end of this workflow, you'll have a system for capturing, organizing, and retrieving personal knowledge. You'll learn to structure notes so you can find information when you need it, and use AI to summarize and connect your notes. Spend less time searching for information and more time using it.

## Who This Is For
This workflow is for anyone who:
- Takes lots of notes but can never find them later.
- Reads books, articles, or takes courses and wants to retain insights.
- Needs to synthesize information from multiple sources.
- Wants a "second brain" to store and retrieve knowledge.

## Inputs
- **Notes and sources:** Your existing notes, highlights, bookmarks, and information you want to capture.
- **Note-taking tool:** An app like Notion, Obsidian, Evernote, or even a folder of text files.
- **Organizational system:** Categories, tags, or structure for your knowledge base.
- **(Optional) AI assistant:** To help summarize, categorize, and connect notes.

## Step-by-Step Workflow

### Step 1: Choose Your Tool
Select a note-taking app that supports:
- Easy capture (quick notes from anywhere)
- Search (find notes by keyword)
- Organization (folders, tags, or links)
- Long-term reliability (your notes should last for years)

Popular options: Notion, Obsidian, Roam Research, Evernote, Apple Notes.

### Step 2: Create Your Structure
Set up basic organization:
- **Areas:** Broad categories (Work, Personal, Learning, Projects)
- **Topics:** Specific subjects (Marketing, Programming, Health, Finance)
- **Types:** Kinds of notes (Book notes, Meeting notes, Ideas, Reference)

Don't over-complicate – start simple and evolve as needed.

### Step 3: Capture Everything
Get in the habit of capturing:
- Insights from books and articles (highlight and export to your tool)
- Meeting notes and key takeaways
- Random ideas and thoughts
- Useful references and resources

The key is consistent capture – it doesn't need to be perfect, just captured.

### Step 4: Process Your Notes Regularly
Set aside time (weekly or bi-weekly) to process captured notes:
- Add context: Why did you save this? How might you use it?
- Add tags or links: Connect to related notes.
- Summarize: Distill long notes into key points.

### Step 5: Use AI to Summarize and Connect (Optional)
AI can help process notes:
- "Summarize this article/book chapter in 5 key points: [paste content]"
- "I have these notes on [topic]. What are the main themes?"
- "How does this note on [A] connect to my earlier note on [B]?"
- "Create flashcard-style questions from these notes for review."

### Step 6: Review and Resurface
Your knowledge base is useless if you never look at it. Build review habits:
- **Weekly review:** Skim recent notes.
- **Spaced repetition:** Use flashcards for important facts.
- **Search when needed:** When working on a topic, search your notes first.
- **Random rediscovery:** Some apps show "on this day" or random notes.

### Step 7: Iterate on Your System
As your knowledge base grows:
- Merge duplicate topics.
- Split overly broad categories.
- Archive outdated notes.
- Refine your tagging system.

Your system should evolve with your needs.

## Prompt Templates
- "Summarize this article in 5 bullet points, highlighting actionable insights: [paste article]"
- "I have book notes scattered across these entries: [paste notes]. Consolidate them into a coherent summary."
- "What are the main themes across these notes about [topic]? [paste notes]"
- "Create 5 review questions based on this material: [paste content]"
- "I'm researching [topic]. Based on my notes, what gaps in my knowledge should I fill? [paste notes]"
- "Suggest tags for this note: [paste note content]"

## Common Mistakes
- **Not capturing:** If you don't write it down, you'll forget it. Capture liberally.
- **Over-organizing:** Spending more time organizing than using your notes. Keep it simple.
- **Never reviewing:** A knowledge base you never revisit is just digital hoarding.
- **No connections:** Notes in isolation are less valuable. Link related ideas.
- **Tool hopping:** Switching tools constantly. Pick one and commit for at least a year.
- **Perfectionism:** Notes don't need to be perfect. Imperfect notes you can find beat perfect notes that don't exist.
